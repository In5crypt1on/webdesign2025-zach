Whales, magnificent marine mammals, are broadly divided into two major groups: Baleen Whales (Mysticeti) and Toothed Whales (Odontoceti). Baleen whales, such as the colossal Blue Whale and the Humpback Whale, are filter feeders, utilizing brush-like plates of baleen in their mouths to strain plankton and small fish from the water. In contrast, Toothed whales, which include dolphins, porpoises, Orcas (Killer Whales), and Sperm Whales, possess sharp teeth and are active hunters, using sophisticated echolocation to navigate and capture prey. This fundamental difference in feeding structure dictates their behavior, social patterns, and migratory routes, contributing to the incredible ecological and physical diversity observed across all whale species inhabiting the world's oceans
